🛡️ Solo Traveler Essentials

  • Cash is King: International Visa/Mastercard won't work. Bring USD/Euro cash to exchange for Rubles immediately. We recommend using Komkom Bank for reliable exchange rates.
  • Stay Connected: Buy a local SIM card (MTS or Megafon) at SVO airport. It's cheap and essential for Yandex Go.
  • Offline Translation: Download Yandex Translate with the offline Russian pack. You can use the "Camera" mode to read menus.
  • Documents: Always carry your physical passport and the Migration Card (small white slip given at immigration). DO NOT LOSE THE MIGRATION CARD; you cannot check into hotels without it.
  • Safe Routes: Stick to the pinned Yandex Maps locations. Murmansk and St. Petersburg are very safe, but avoid wandering into industrial dock areas alone at night.

💡 Quick Pro-Tips

  • Plugs: Russia uses European Type C/F (Two-pin).
  • Water: Do not drink tap water; buy bottled water.
  • Tipping: 10-15% is standard in restaurants.
  • Photography: Avoid taking photos of police, military, or airport security.
  • Emergency: Dial 112 for all emergency services.

🍴 Gourmet Russian & Arctic Flavors

Don't leave Russia without trying these regional specialties!

❄️ Arctic Delicacies

  • 🥩 Venison (Reindeer): Try it as a steak or in a stew.
  • 🦀 King Crab: Murmansk is world-famous for its fresh crab.
  • 🫐 Cloudberries: A rare "Arctic Gold" berry, great in tea or jam.
  • 🐟 Cod/Halibut: Freshly caught from the Barents Sea.

🏙️ City Classics

  • 🍩 Pyshki: Traditional Soviet-style donuts (Must-try in SPB!).
  • 🥘 Beef Stroganoff: A creamy classic born in St. Petersburg.
  • 🥟 Pelmeni: Delicious meat dumplings served with sour cream.
  • 🥞 Blini: Russian pancakes with caviar or sweet condensed milk.

💡 Pro Tip: Most hotel restaurants in Murmansk serve excellent local seafood if you don't want to wander far!
